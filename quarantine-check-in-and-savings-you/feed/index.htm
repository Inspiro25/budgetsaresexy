Financially, my fiancée and I are doing ok, comparatively speaking; I’m marginally less comfortable than I would have been if this happened, say, a year or two ago, since we bought our first house about 10 months ago, and in the last 6 months have started to see some financial outlay for the wedding (which not coincidentally are the reasons I haven’t posted in a while, watching these two things put a “dent” in my closeness to FIRE status briefly depressed me, lol), but ultimately I remind myself that I’m blessed to be in the position I’m in, thanks to my financial habits over the past 10 years, and my problems are small compared to many folk.(Too, I learnt my lesson from panicking during the market correction of 2011 (a year after I started investing), so psychologically I feel relatively well equipped).

I work in accounting, so I’ve been deemed essential, which I’m ok with, as our office isn’t large, and we’ve stopped face to face client meetings..and truthfully if my fiancée were both stuck at home 24/7 we probably wouldn’t make it to the wedding, haha. :)

She currently isn’t working, as she works for a craft brewery (they’re still doing to-go crowlers and growlers a few hours each day, but they’ve had to trim the staff to only ownership, which is 4 folk…though currently all signs point to them staying afloat well enough to bring back the full staff once this passes), but she got her first unemployment check yesterday, which truthfully is a big help, because though small, it means we don’t currently have to drain the emergency fund to pay the bills.

We don’t have any services such as daycare (since it keeps getting mentioned) that we’re faced with the question of paying while they’re closed down, but, thanks to my fiancée’s job, we’ve gained a new appreciation for local small businesses, so we’re trying to do what we can to support those around us (which mostly involves drinking good beer and eating good food from mom and pop places, so it’s not what I’d call a hardship, lol).

I also think it’s nice to see more folk out just taking walks, communicating (even if it’s just texting more often), and making a point to try and support the folk around them.
