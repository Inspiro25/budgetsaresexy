Hey Amanda! I’ll answer here, AND we’ll discuss over beer :)

– From my understanding it doesn’t matter where the “custodian” lives. Only where the kid lives. The account is in his/her name, so rules apply to the state they are in when they turn legal age.

– Yes I guess it could be a surprise, BUT: a) you need the kid’s Social Security Number. So probably another adult has to know and b) you could be screwing up the kid’s chance of getting financial aid later in life. In hindsight, I wish I discussed this more with the parents first to see how my gift fits into the kid’s overall “inheritance & gift strategy”. Make sense?

– I don’t think they can access anything early! But what would an under 18y/o need that much money for in the first place? haha.

– Paying taxes starts the moment there are realized capital gains. So it depends on what funds you are invested in within the account. Consider this just like a regular brokerage account. You pay taxes when you sell stock higher than what you bought it for, and make gains. If you never sell stock for 20 years, you pay no taxes. As soon as you sell something, it becomes “realized”. Make sense? This might be an over beer discussion!
