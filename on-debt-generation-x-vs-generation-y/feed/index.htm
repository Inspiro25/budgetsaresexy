]]> By: Mary Anne @ BillGuard https://budgetsaresexy.vercel.app/on-debt-generation-x-vs-generation-y/#comment-117844 Thu, 28 Mar 2013 20:04:37 +0000 https://staging.budgetsaresexy.vercel.app/?p=32360#comment-117844 The stats are interesting. I wonder why Gen X and Gen Y are so different. It makes sense, as many commenters have noted, that many people in Gen Y haven’t yet purchased houses and haven’t yet gone to grad school, so it makes sense that their mortgage and student loan debt would be lower. (On the other hand, Gen Y has had LESS time to repay their student loans and mortgages, which is an argument for why those balances should be higher).

The real mystery is the credit-card debt. I can’t figure out why Gen X’s would be so much higher than Gen Y’s, given that Gen X is, as an aggregate, presumably making a much higher income. (They’ve had more time in the workforce). My only hypothesis is that Gen Y became financially conservative after witnessing the recession. They’re also young enough that they didn’t lose their home equity or their jobs during the recession (because they hadn’t bought homes yet and they hadn’t finished school yet). That’s just a guess, though.
