]]> By: Sheri D. https://budgetsaresexy.vercel.app/operation-give-back-in-real-life-results/#comment-459342 Mon, 27 Mar 2023 14:22:18 +0000 https://budgetsaresexy.vercel.app/?p=67255#comment-459342 I fell into this same idea last year…What started out as a local community “Adopt-a-Family” for Christmas turned into the realization that the need doesn’t end with the holidays.

As I discovered by talking to their clinician, our state support system has very little exit strategy aside from subsidized housing when foster “kids” over eighteen (and they are still kids at this point) age out of support.

When these young adults arrive in group housing they are given rudimentary supplies…one set of sheets and towels, one pillow…you get the idea.

After they are approved for independent living, they leave with little more than that. No pots, pans, dishes, cutlery, bathroom supplies – no household basics and oftentimes only outgrown clothing that is too many years old.

These young people have survived the foster care program only to exit the system having to fend for themselves. They are virtually invisible to society.

When you consider the number of items trashed daily all around us I thought…Imagine what we could accomplish with the concept of pairing donated home goods with these young people ready for their first-ever homes.

The week of Christmas Cincinnati was expecting a big snow storm and plummeting temperatures. I was off on Thursday the 23rd and thought I would get to the grocery early to beat the crowds. Everybody else had the same bright idea. Staffing was short and the lines to the checkout literally snaked to the back of the building. I asked the person in front of me to advance my cart for me.
