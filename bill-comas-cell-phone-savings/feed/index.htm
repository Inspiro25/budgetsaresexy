Already canceled one audiobook subscription (I hate that these days is next to impossible to buy a single electronic thing, everything is subscription based) and next will cancel/switch dns service of one unused domain.

However, as a Finn I find where the savings come is in part quite different. For instance, I find it ridiculous how much Americans have to pay for a basic right such as the Internet ;) our plans here are €10-20 a month, with unlimited 4G data and calls. (Phones you buy separately so no scammy bundles.) Service level covers every part of the country except the wilderness. Also health and retirement plans are part of salary and universities are free. (The tax levels are the same as in New York.) That said, I will definitely want to be able to choose when to retire (not at gov-standard 67) and maybe start my own business again.

I will definitely use this separate account idea for sold stuff – we’ve sold a lot of unused stuff and just used that to lower our daily grocery bill but that way it seems to fritter away.

A pro tip for those who have less discipline (like us): we kept a couple accounts in another bank after switching banks some years ago. I put all my side earnings + joint extra savings there. We don’t see them when we check our normal accounts and morgage -> no temptations to use it and nice surprises whe we do check the accounts! (Also investments are in separate, cheaper investment bank.)
