Love it. I’ve done some proofing and paid for some as well. I can see why a lot of bloggers said no though. It’s an expense that doesn’t necessarily generate ROI. People expect a touch of grammar and spelling issues on blogs and are generally forgiving – but someone seeking a professional service would be less so.

Learning to adapt proofing styles is key. I did some freelance work for a college friend of mine who started a web design service – he paid me well and I could do it kind of whenever as long as I got it done by a particular date. But I learned quickly editing for business clients is different than newspaper editing – one has to use a lot of diplomacy and also really understand the audience. The first site I worked on had a lot of jargon and it was crucial to know if their potential clients understood/expected the jargon or not. Eventually my friend got a pretty sweet university gig so shut down the business but it was nice work while it lasted.

Proofing/copyediting can be a great side hustle. Still not as good as my photography side hustle but so far it’s second place in the rate I’ve earned.
