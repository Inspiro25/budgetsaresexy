]]> By: Menard Solve https://budgetsaresexy.vercel.app/ben-franklin-the-og-of-fire/#comment-285029 Mon, 28 Oct 2019 18:18:14 +0000 https://staging.budgetsaresexy.vercel.app/?p=62059#comment-285029 If you google “Benjamin Franklin Fire Movement”, one really cool dude wrote about how Franklin pioneered the movement. His points are:

1. He treated his debt as an emergency (ala MMM).
2. He lived frugally in terms of time and money (ala Vicky Robin).
3. He understood risk vs reward.
4. He became F.I. and retired early to pursue other passions.
5. He leveraged the power of compounding.

Not only was he playing with fire (with the lightning rod and the Franklin stove), he was living the FIRE lifestyle!

In the 19th century it was fairly common for young men to go ‘abroad’ or into business to seek “a competence”. In other words, generating a big stash of money they could live in luxury on without needing to work.

Usually this was around $100k (remember this is back in the 1800’s!), but what I find most surprising was that most of them never stayed “retired” for very long. After a few years of relaxing they were usually back at it — buying a business, shipping goods across the ocean, and the like.
