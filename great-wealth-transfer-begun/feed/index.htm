I wanted to start off my expressing my condolences for your loss. Nothing can replace the loss of a parent. Vanguard customer service reps are great, but their knowledge of tax laws around inherited assets at both the state and federal level will be quite limited. I would still highly suggest talking to a local tax accountant who deals with estates. There are some very specific rules over how different assets are valued at the time of inheritance (known as “basis”). In the example of stock, I believe your basis would be determined on the date of inheritance regardless of whether the values have gained or lost value since purchase. But it can also get more complicated than this with other types of assets (real estate, businesses, etc). Your inherited basis isn’t always determined at the date of inheritance. For example, when I quickly looked this topic up to refresh my memory I came across an exception where the executor of an estate can set the date of inheritance 6mos after the owner passed away. Depending on the timing, you could actually shield more of those inherited funds from taxation because you might have a higher inherited basis. Best of luck with this process.
